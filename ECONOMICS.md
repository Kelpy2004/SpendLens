# SpendLens Economics

SpendLens is free at the audit layer. The business is Credex conversion: when the audit finds meaningful savings, Credex sells an AI spend cleanup and governance subscription.

The paid product should be priced against recovered waste, not against "another SaaS dashboard." If SpendLens finds $1,500/month in savings and Credex captures the cleanup, a $1,500/month subscription is easy to defend because the customer is roughly cash-neutral in month one and cleaner after that.

## Offer And Pricing

Free: SpendLens audit, public result URL, and email confirmation.

Paid: Credex AI Spend Control.

Base price: $1,500/month on an annual contract, or $18,000 ARR per customer.

Success kicker: 10% of first-year verified savings above $12,000/year. I would not lead with this on the website, but it gives room for larger accounts where Credex directly negotiates renewals.

Target customer: companies spending at least $5,000/month on AI tools and APIs, or companies with at least $500/month in defensible savings from the audit.

## Funnel Math

The first real funnel should look like this:

| Step | Rate | Count from 10,000 visitors | Notes |
| --- | ---: | ---: | --- |
| Visits to completed audit | 22% | 2,200 | The form has a clear value prop and no email wall. |
| Completed audit to captured lead | 18% | 396 | Email capture happens after value is shown. |
| Captured lead to qualified opportunity | 35% | 139 | Qualified means savings above $500/mo or AI spend above $5k/mo. |
| Qualified opportunity to consultation | 45% | 63 | Strong because the audit already shows money. |
| Consultation to paid Credex | 28% | 18 | Conservative for a productized cleanup service. |

At $18,000 ARR, 18 customers equals $324,000 ARR per 10,000 high-intent visitors.

## CAC By Channel

I count founder time at $80/hour. It is not paid media, but it is still a cost.

| Channel | Monthly activity | Cost assumption | Expected customers | CAC |
| --- | ---: | ---: | ---: | ---: |
| Reddit and community teardown posts | 20 hours/month | $1,600 labor | 2 customers | $800 |
| Founder/finance outbound | 30 hours/month + enrichment | $2,400 labor + $150 tools | 3 customers | $850 |
| Fractional CFO referrals | 10 partner calls/month | $800 labor + 10% first-year referral fee | 4 customers | $1,800 cash-equivalent |
| Benchmark report and LinkedIn distribution | 25 hours/month | $2,000 labor | 2 customers | $1,000 |
| Product Hunt launch month | 40 hours once | $3,200 labor | 6 customers | $533 |

Blended early CAC target: about $1,100. With $18,000 ARR and an 85% gross margin software/service mix, first-year gross profit is $15,300. That leaves plenty of room for founder-led sales and a light-touch onboarding call.

## Payback And Margin

If a customer pays $1,500/month and gross margin is 85%, monthly gross profit is $1,275.

With $1,100 blended CAC, payback is $1,100 / $1,275 = 0.86 months.

That payback only holds if Credex onboarding stays productized. If every customer needs a bespoke consulting project, gross margin falls under 60%, onboarding becomes the bottleneck, and the company is a services firm with a nice lead magnet. The product line needs templates: renewal checklist, seat policy, vendor owner map, API usage review, and monthly savings report.

## Path To $1M ARR In 18 Months

$1M ARR at $18,000 ARR per customer requires 56 customers.

Required pace:

- Months 1 to 3: 6 customers total, mostly founder-led and referral-led.
- Months 4 to 9: 24 more customers, 4 per month from audits, outbound, and fractional CFOs.
- Months 10 to 18: 26 more customers, about 3 per month, with better conversion because the benchmark report and customer proof are stronger.

Traffic needed over 18 months:

56 customers / 28% close from consultation = 200 consultations.

200 consultations / 45% qualified-to-consultation = 445 qualified opportunities.

445 qualified opportunities / 35% lead-to-qualified = 1,272 captured leads.

1,272 leads / 18% audit-to-lead = 7,067 completed audits.

7,067 audits / 22% visit-to-audit = 32,123 targeted visitors.

That is about 1,785 targeted visitors per month. This is not consumer-scale traffic. It is a focused operator audience, which is why community content, referrals, and targeted outbound can plausibly get there before paid search.

The failure mode is weak qualification. If most audits show $40/month in savings, users may like the product but will not buy Credex. The business only works if the audit consistently surfaces enough money or enough governance pain to justify a sales conversation.
