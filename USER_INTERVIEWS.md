# SpendLens User Interviews

---

## Interview 1 — A.M.

**Role:** CS Final Year Student (Interned at SmartQ as Software Dev)
**Date:** May 11, 2026
**Monthly AI spend:** ₹2,000–4,000

A.M. is my friend. He uses AI quite often — mostly Claude and ChatGPT since he's into development and needs help with his coding projects. The conversation happened in Hindi so I translated it here.

When he first saw the website he wasn't impressed with the basic UI. I changed it a few times after and made it look better and easier to understand for new people.

I asked him how he would benefit from my site. He said he uses AI tools quite a lot now since we're in final year, and since he's in college he needs to monitor his expenses — so it's a perfect place to get the budget set and even reduce it.

**Quotes:**
> "Fix the UI brother."

> "A few more upgrades and I will be the first one to use this app. I really need it."

I also asked him — if the site told you your spend was totally fine and you weren't overpaying, would you trust that answer or would it feel like the tool missed something? He said he'd trust it only if it showed the actual usage data behind the call and where the pricing numbers came from. Just a "you're good" with nothing backing it up wouldn't feel reliable to him.

**Most surprising thing:** The feedback was almost all about design. He understood the value straight away — the problem was just that the UI looked rough so it didn't feel trustworthy.

**What changed because of this:** The UI was changed after he advised. Which is why now it's easier to understand.

## Interview 2 — R.K.

**Role:** Senior Analyst
**Company:** TCS
**Date:** May 12, 2026
**Monthly AI spend:** ~₹3,500

R.K. is my uncle. He's always my go-to when I have doubts — good thing he's in the engineering field which made it easy to ask questions about the site.

I asked him when he uses AI and what for. He said he uses AI just to make work easier and faster, not to trust it blindly, but it's really good for the boring parts. Anything important he still checks himself.

His company provides an AI tool for most of his work which is free. What he personally pays for is Canva and Granola — for presentations and meeting notes.

I asked him to go through the site. His first reaction was positive — the interface was easy to get to. He was impressed with the idea, though I told him later it's actually an assignment. I asked if he'd recommend it to his colleagues. He said definitely, after a few more things are done — mainly making the final report feel trustworthy enough for a CTO to forward.

**Quotes:**
> "The interface is easy to get to... good work beta."

> "Still needs work to be done."

I asked him the same — if the site said your spend is totally fine, would you trust it? He said not straight away. He'd want to see what alternatives were compared against, and what assumptions the tool was making. If those aren't listed clearly he'd assume the tool just didn't check properly rather than his spend actually being okay.

**Most surprising thing:** "I didn't know you can build sites now." (He was being humorous.)

**What changed because of this:** It changed the thinking from "blank calculator" to needing a proper, shareable output. The report needs to feel credible enough to forward, not just useful for the person running it.

## Interview 3 — S.B.

**Role:** Software Developer / Blogger
**Company:** Reducate.ai
**Date:** May 12, 2026
**Monthly AI spend:** ₹10,000+

Since I've been looking for internships recently I've come across a lot of people on LinkedIn. Sakshi was one of them so I thought of asking her about the website.

I asked what AI tools she uses — she uses Claude as her main tool for coding. How much does she spend? Quite a lot since she's on the Pro plan.

I asked: if you're spending this much on AI tools, would something that helps you track and compare be useful? She said there was a time she needed exactly that — she wanted to check if her spending was worth it and compare alternatives. But now that she's on Claude Max she's just stuck with it. It's too good.

I asked her to run through the site. She said she liked the easy design. She asked if anything else is currently doing this.

Then she gave a recommendation — if the site could also tell users whether they're getting value from what they're paying for. Basically, are engineers actually using these tools enough to justify the spend? ROI reports.

**Quotes:**
> "I like your idea Aryan."

> "I like the easy design you made."

> "If your site shows ROI reports for AI usage it'd be so much better."

**Most surprising thing:** She spends more than ₹10,000 a month on AI. That's a lot for an individual developer — but her reasoning made sense, the tool pays for itself in time saved.

**What this could change:** ROI reporting — showing if users are getting enough value from what they're paying for, not just whether they're on the right plan.
