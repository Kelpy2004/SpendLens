# SpendLens GTM

The first user is not "anyone who uses AI tools." That is too soft. The first user is the Head of Finance, COO, or founder at a 10 to 80 person B2B SaaS company that moved fast on AI tools in the last year and now has Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, and API bills scattered across cards and employee reimbursements.

The wedge is simple: finance wants control, engineering does not want procurement theater, and founders want runway. SpendLens should feel like a neutral audit, not a vendor replacement pitch.

## Exact Target User

Primary buyer: Head of Finance or COO at a Seed to Series B SaaS company with 10 to 80 employees, $1M to $15M ARR, and at least $2,000/month in AI tooling spend.

Primary champion: engineering manager or founder who knows the tool sprawl is real but does not want finance to blindly cancel the tools people actually use.

The best early segment is AI-native SaaS companies and dev-tool startups. They have high AI adoption, visible tool overlap, and enough team size for seat waste to hurt. A five-person agency can use the audit, but it will not create Credex-level urgency. A 300-person company has a longer procurement cycle. The sweet spot is the team that is big enough to leak money and small enough to fix it this week.

- Finance Alliance Slack: free community for CFOs, VPs of Finance, finance directors, and FP&A operators.
- CFO Connect: useful for European finance operators and startup finance leads.
- LinkedIn comments on posts by fractional CFOs, SaaS finance consultants, and finance creators who write about runway, burn multiple, and vendor audits.

Founder and operator communities:

- `r/SaaS`, especially posts about tool stacks, pricing, runway, and "what are you paying for?"
- `r/startups`, but only for useful teardown posts, not launch spam.
- `r/microsaas` for founder-led companies that track every dollar.
- `r/ExperiencedDevs` and `r/devops` for the engineering side of AI coding-tool decisions.
- Indie Hackers and Product Hunt discussions when the angle is "here is what 50 audits taught us," not "please try my app."

Outbound lists:

- Recently funded Seed and Series A SaaS companies from public funding announcements.
- Companies hiring finance roles while also hiring engineers, which usually means spend is growing faster than controls.
- Startups posting engineering job descriptions that mention Cursor, Copilot, Claude, or AI coding workflows.

## First 100 Users In 30 Days With $0 Paid Budget

Day 1 to 3: run 10 manual audits for founder friends, indie hackers, and any SaaS operator willing to share screenshots of tool spend. Do not ask for testimonials yet. Ask what felt obvious, what felt wrong, and which recommendation they would actually act on.

Day 4 to 7: publish a teardown post in `r/SaaS`: "I audited 10 startup AI tool stacks. The boring leak was not API spend, it was seat overlap." Include anonymized numbers, screenshots of SpendLens, and a clear line that the audit is free. Reply to every serious comment with a mini-audit, not a pitch.

Day 8 to 12: DM 75 founders and finance leads with a specific opener: "I noticed you are hiring engineers and probably have Cursor/Copilot/ChatGPT overlap. Want me to run a free AI spend audit and send the teardown?" This should target people with a real reason, not scraped spam.

Day 13 to 16: post in CFO Chat and Finance Alliance only after asking moderators for permission. The post should be a useful prompt: "What AI tools are slipping through reimbursements?" Offer to turn anonymized answers into a benchmark report for the group.

Day 17 to 21: create 20 public example audits using realistic stacks: "10-person dev team on Cursor Business + Copilot Enterprise", "Claude Team below the seat floor", "ChatGPT Team plus API overlap." Share one per day on LinkedIn and in founder communities as a lesson, not a launch post.

Day 22 to 26: recruit 10 fractional CFOs as distribution partners. Give them a co-branded audit link and a simple promise: every qualified lead they send gets a free audit; if Credex closes a paid cleanup, they get a referral fee after revenue arrives.

Day 27 to 30: ship a short benchmark PDF from the first 50 audits. Title it "The 2026 Startup AI Spend Leak Report." The goal is not polish. The goal is a credible artifact that gives finance people a reason to forward SpendLens internally.

The first 100 users should come from roughly 40 Reddit/community audits, 30 outbound audits, 20 fractional CFO referrals, and 10 warm network audits. If the product cannot get 100 free audits from that motion, paid acquisition will not save it.

## The Unfair Distribution Channel

Credex already has relationships with companies that overspent on AI infrastructure — that's literally the business. Every company that bought credits through Credex is a warm lead for SpendLens, and every SpendLens audit that surfaces $500+/month in savings is a warm lead for Credex. No generic spend tool has that loop.

The specific move only Credex can make: email existing credit buyers and say "we noticed your team bought $X in credits last quarter — want a free audit to see if your app subscriptions are aligned?" That's not a cold outreach. It's a vendor following up with something genuinely useful. Open rates will be completely different from a cold campaign.

Fractional CFOs are the second piece. There are thousands of them serving exactly the 10 to 80 person SaaS companies that SpendLens is built for. A fractional CFO who can drop a co-branded audit link into a client meeting is offering a real deliverable, not just a referral. Credex runs the infrastructure, the CFO gets a client touchpoint, and both share the qualified leads. No other tool in this space has the vendor-side positioning to make that referral fee credible.

## What Week-1 Traction Looks Like If This Works

Rough targets for a working launch week:

- 300–500 unique visitors from the Reddit post and warm network
- 80–120 completed audits (20–25% visit-to-audit)
- 12–20 email captures on the result page (15–18% capture rate)
- 3–5 audits surfacing over $500/month that trigger the Credex callout
- At least 1 inbound asking about the Credex consultation

Under 50 completed audits in week one means the distribution angle isn't working and needs to change before anything else. Over 100 audits but under 10 email captures means the result page isn't creating enough urgency — the problem is the product, not the traffic.
