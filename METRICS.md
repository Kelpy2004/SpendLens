# SpendLens Metrics

## North Star

North Star metric: verified monthly AI savings accepted by users.

This is not the same as "savings found." A rule engine can always find theoretical savings by telling everyone to downgrade. That would be useless. The metric only counts savings when a user saves the audit, confirms the recommendation is plausible, or books a Credex cleanup call. The product wins when it changes a real spend decision without damaging the team's workflow.

Example: if SpendLens finds $1,200/month in savings but the customer says only $800/month is actionable because the rest is tied to a contract, the North Star gets $800.

## Input Metrics

Completed audits per week.

This measures whether the intake is clear enough and whether distribution is working. A visitor who does not finish the audit has not received value, and a partial audit does not create a credible lead.

Lead capture rate after result.

This should be measured only after the result page loads. If 1,000 users see results and 180 leave an email, that is an 18% capture rate. If the rate is too low, the result page is either not useful enough or the email ask feels too heavy.

Qualified savings rate.

This is the percentage of completed audits with either more than $500/month in defensible savings or more than $5,000/month in total AI spend. This is the core business quality metric. A lot of small audits can make analytics look busy while producing no Credex revenue.

## Operating Dashboard

Every week, I would track:

| Metric | Healthy early signal | Why it matters |
| --- | ---: | --- |
| Completed audits | 100/week by week 6 | Enough volume to learn from real stacks. |
| Result-to-lead capture | 15% to 25% | Shows users trust the result enough to continue. |
| Qualified savings rate | 25% to 40% | Shows Credex has a real sales pool. |
| Consultation booking rate | 35%+ of qualified leads | Shows the audit creates urgency. |
| Paid conversion | 20%+ of consultations | Shows the paid offer is priced and packaged correctly. |

## Pivot Triggers

Pivot the target segment if fewer than 15% of completed audits show $500/month in defendable savings after the first 300 audits. That means the current audience likes the idea but does not have enough pain.

Pivot the lead capture if result-to-lead capture stays below 10% after 1,000 result views. The likely fix is not a bigger popup. It is a better result page, a stronger sample audit, or a clearer reason to receive the confirmation email.

Pivot the business model if qualified leads book consultations below 20%. That means people believe the audit but do not see enough reason to talk. The next test would be a self-serve paid report at $99 to $299 instead of a Credex consultation.

Pivot away from Credex-led cleanup if paid conversion is below 15% after 50 consultations with qualified accounts. At that point, SpendLens may be more valuable as a benchmarking and renewal-prep product than as a front door to procurement services.

Do not pivot because one channel is quiet for a week. Pivot when the math says the audience, ask, or monetization path is structurally wrong.
